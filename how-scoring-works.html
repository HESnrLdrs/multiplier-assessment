How Scoring Works

A detailed explanation of how your assessment results are calculated

Quick Summary: Your assessment uses a 5-point Likert scale for each question. Responses are converted to a 0-100 scale for each dimension, and these are multiplied together to create your Transition Capacity Score.

Step-by-Step Scoring Process

1Answer Each Question

You respond to 15 questions (5 per dimension) using a 5-point scale:

  • 1 = Strongly Disagree
  • 2 = Disagree
  • 3 = Neutral
  • 4 = Agree
  • 5 = Strongly Agree

2Calculate Raw Score per Dimension

Your responses for each dimension are added together:

Example - Motivation Questions:
Q1: 4 + Q2: 5 + Q3: 3 + Q4: 4 + Q5: 5 = 21 points
Range: Minimum 5 points (all 1s) to Maximum 25 points (all 5s)

3Convert to 0-100 Scale

Raw scores are converted to a 0-100 scale for easier interpretation:

Score = ((Raw - 5) / 20) √ó 100

This formula maps 5-25 points to 0-100

Example Calculation:
Raw Score: 21 points
Converted: ((21 - 5) / 20) √ó 100 = (16 / 20) √ó 100 = 80/100

4Identify Your Constraint

Your constraint is simply the dimension with the lowest score:

  • Motivation: 80/100
  • Learning: 75/100
  • Identity: 60/100 ‚Üê This is your constraint

5Calculate Transition Capacity Score

The three dimension scores are multiplied together to show your overall capacity:

Motivation √ó Learning √ó Identity

Multiplying shows how dimensions work together

Example:
80 √ó 75 √ó 60 = 360,000
Status: "Gaining Traction" (200,001-500,000)

Why We Use a Multiplier

We multiply the three dimensions because transitions require all three working together. If any one dimension is weak, it limits your entire capacity - just like a chain is only as strong as its weakest link.

Real-World Example:

Scenario M L I Capacity
Balanced but low everywhere 50 50 50 125,000
Strong in 2, weak in 1 90 85 30 229,500
Improved the weak link 90 85 50 382,500
All dimensions strong 90 85 80 612,000

Key insight: Improving your constraint (Identity from 30 to 50) increased capacity by 67%. That's why we focus on your weakest dimension first!

Tracking Progress Over Time

We recommend retaking the assessment every 4 weeks. Here's what to look for:

  • Constraint improvement: Is your lowest score increasing?
  • Capacity growth: Is your overall Transition Capacity Score rising?
  • Status advancement: Are you moving from "Building" to "Gaining Traction" to "Making Progress"?
  • Balanced growth: Are all three dimensions strengthening together?

Frequently Asked Questions About Scoring

Why don't you add the scores instead of multiplying?

Addition would hide weaknesses. For example: 90+90+20 = 200 looks okay when added, but 90√ó90√ó20 = 162,000 reveals the problem. The multiplier shows that a weak link limits everything.

Can my Transition Capacity Score go down?

Yes, if you become aware of issues you didn't recognize before. This is actually progress - you're seeing your situation more clearly. It also helps you focus on the right areas.

What's a "good" Transition Capacity Score?

There's no single "good" score - it depends on where you are in your journey. What matters most is: (1) Are you improving over time? (2) Are you addressing your constraint? (3) Do you feel more capable?

Why is the maximum score 1,000,000?

100 √ó 100 √ó 100 = 1,000,000. This large range helps capture meaningful differences in transition capacity and shows progress clearly.
